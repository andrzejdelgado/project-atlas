## The problem

Senior designers who promote into staff/principal often lose what got
them there. They drift toward meetings, lose touch with the product,
and start being measured by leverage they haven't built yet.

## The frame

Three modes, in roughly equal proportion:

- **Operator (≈40%)** — still shipping. Prototypes, design-engineering
  work, the occasional surface-level pixel push. This is the keel.
- **Mentor (≈30%)** — multipliers, not blockers. Time spent making
  three other designers better than time spent building one feature
  yourself.
- **Strategist (≈30%)** — long-horizon work. Roadmap, architecture,
  hiring, system decisions that compound for years.

## What worked

I ran this with about a dozen senior designers over two years. The
pattern that consistently mattered: *protect the operator slot*. The
designers who lost it underperformed within six months, no matter how
strong their strategist work was.

## What didn't

The 40/30/30 split is a starting heuristic, not a target. Half the
people I worked with needed something closer to 50/25/25 in their first
year as staff. Treat the numbers as load-bearing, not law-bearing.
