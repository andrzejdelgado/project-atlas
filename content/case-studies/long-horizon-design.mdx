## Why this exists

Most product platforms calcify between year three and year five. The
people who built them have moved on, the constraints they were solving
for have changed, and the system stops absorbing new requirements.
This is the story of trying to design *against* that fate from the
start.

## The bet

Build for **what we can't predict**. Three principles:

- **Architecture as a question, not an answer.** Each major decision
  shipped with a "this is wrong if…" clause that future teams could
  use to disprove it.
- **One opinionated default, multiple escape hatches.** The platform
  shipped with a single recommended path for every choice — and a
  documented way out for teams who hit its limits.
- **Compounding documentation.** Every PR that changed a behaviour
  also updated the doc that explained the behaviour. No exceptions.

## What changed

Two reorgs and a CEO transition later, the platform is still in use,
still being extended by teams who didn't build it. The documentation
debt is the lowest of any platform I've worked on.

## The honest part

This wasn't free. Every shipped decision took 30–40% longer than the
"just-make-it-work" path. The bet only pays off if the system is
expected to live for years.
