Principal Product Designer with a passion for **design engineering**
and systemic thinking. True collaboration, stakeholder management
and leading projects are my jam. I _care deeply_ about craft and
quality, as well as helping people I work with to reach their full
potential.

I've spent about thirteen years on this. The first half was craft:
shipping product, learning where pixels and milliseconds matter. The
second half has been about leverage: design systems, organizational
architecture, and getting teams to ship like one team.

## What I work on

- Long-horizon design systems — the kind that survive their first
  reorg, then their second.
- Design engineering inside product organizations: prototypes that
  decide arguments, tokens that travel, components that don't lie.
- Long-form writing about how digital infrastructure becomes economic
  infrastructure, and why most companies underestimate the durability
  of their own decisions.

## Mentorship

I mentor on [ADPList](https://adplist.org/) — most often on the move
from senior IC to staff or principal, on portfolio strategy for design
engineering roles, and on building design systems that survive their
first reorg. Sessions are free, and I keep about three or four open
slots a week. Bring a real problem, not a hypothetical.

## How I work

- **Prototype before drawing conclusions.** Most arguments about a
  design evaporate the moment a working version exists.
- **Edit aggressively.** The version of any page that survives the
  first draft is almost never the right one.
- **Stay close to the metal** — typography, motion, color — because
  that's where a product earns its trust.
- **Treat the long horizon as a constraint.** A decision that's right
  for next quarter and wrong for the next decade is usually just a
  decision dressed up.
